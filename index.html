Brexit and the Effect on the British Fishing Industry

Overview of Landings into the UK by UK Vessels vs Foreign Vessels and Landings Abroad by UK Vessels

When looking at the data of UK landings by UK vessels, UK landings by foreign vessels and foreign landings by UK vessels, we can see that right after the EU referendum there was a sudden spike in landings in all three categories followed by a steep descent in August and September. This decline in landings happens a little bit earlier compared to the previous two years, when the October seems to be the peak month of the year. The decline in UK landings by foreign vessels is especially significant as it hits its lowest value in nearly two years. It will be interesting to see if the negative trend will continue for the next months or if the landings will pick up like they have done in the early months of the previous two years. Also of interest is the distribution of the three categories. The landings (quantity in tonnes) in the UK by UK vessels is by far greater than the UK landings by foreign vessels. This indicates that the UK does actually have a lot of control of their waters in terms of landings. Even more, landings abroad by UK landings is significantly higher than UK landings by foreign vessels. It will be interesting to see whether other countries such as France and Denmark will impose restrictions on how much UK vessels can fish and sell in their ports as a result of the referendum.
